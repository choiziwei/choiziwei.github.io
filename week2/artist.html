Mrs. GREEN APPLE

Biography

Mrs. GREEN APPLE is a Japanese three-member band consisting of Motoki Ohmori (Vo/Gt), Hiloto Wakai (Gt), Ryoka Fujisawa (Key). Formed in 2013 and making their major debut in 2015 with the mini-album Variety, the band quickly rose to prominence through consistent album releases, sold-out tours, and a growing catalog of hit songs. After completing “Phase 1” with the chart-topping greatest hits album 5 in 2020, they entered a hiatus before returning in 2022 to begin “Phase 2” with the mini-album Unity, the comeback concert Utopia, and major tie-in songs including “I'm Invincible” and “Soranji.” Their success accelerated with the album Antenna (2023), arena and dome tours, multiple Japan Record Awards, and their first appearance on the NHK Kohaku Uta Gassen. Throughout 2024 and 2025, they released numerous hit songs such as “Lilac,” “Dear,” “Columbus,” “Darling,” “KUSUSHIKI,” and “Heaven,” while breaking records with stadium, arena, and five-dome tours. Their anniversary best album 10 surpassed one million sales, becoming the first rock album in the Reiwa era to achieve the milestone. Recognized as one of Japan's most influential contemporary acts, the band has accumulated over 16 billion streams, won three consecutive Japan Record Awards, ranked among the IFPI Global Top-Selling Artists of 2025, and continues to expand its global presence. Entering “Phase 3” in 2026 with the single “lulu.” and “Wind and Town,” Mrs. GREEN APPLE remains one of the most successful and innovative bands in modern Japanese music.
